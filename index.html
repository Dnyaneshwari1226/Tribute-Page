A. P. J ABDUL KALAM

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Aval Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, India, was a prominent Indian scientist and politician. He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Kalam earned recognition as a leading aerospace engineer,contributing significantly to India's space and missile programs. Notably, he played a key role in the development of ballistic missile technology.Apart from his scientific endeavors, Kalam was widely admired for his inspirational speeches, emphasizing education, innovation, and moral values. He authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds. " Kalam's commitment to empowering youth earned him the nickname "People's President." He passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy continues to influence and inspire people across the globe.
